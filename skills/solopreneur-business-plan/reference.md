# Solopreneur Business Plan — Reference

Use this when you need concrete prompts for clarifying questions or a checklist for the Reality check.

## Example Clarifying Questions (by topic)

**Idea & offer**
- What exactly are you selling (product, service, or both)? In one sentence, what outcome does the customer get?
- Is this a new offer or an evolution of something you already do?

**Customer & validation**
- Who is the specific customer (role, industry, size, geography)? Not "small businesses" but e.g. "B2B SaaS founders in the US, 5–20 employees, already spending on marketing."
- How do you know they have this problem? (Conversations, surveys, your own experience, competitors?)
- What do they use today to solve it, and what do they complain about?

**You & constraints**
- What's your relevant experience or track record in this space?
- How much time can you put in per week/month? Any hard deadlines (e.g. quit job in 12 months)?
- What's your budget or runway (if any) for tools, ads, or living expenses while ramping?

**Money & model**
- How do you want to charge (one-time, retainer, subscription, project)?
- Do you have a price in mind or a range? What's it based on (competitors, value, cost)?
- What are your main costs (tools, subcontractors, marketing, your time)?

**Competition & differentiation**
- Who else does this? (Names or types of players.)
- Why would a customer pick you over them (price, quality, niche, relationship)?

## Reality Check Checklist

Before finalizing the plan, ensure the **Reality check** section addresses:

- [ ] **Market/need** — Is there real evidence of demand, or is it mostly hope? What would disprove it?
- [ ] **Competition** — Why would customers switch or choose you? What if incumbents react?
- [ ] **Unit economics** — Do revenue per customer and CAC/fulfillment cost leave room for profit at solopreneur scale?
- [ ] **Capacity** — Can one person actually deliver and sell at the volume the plan assumes?
- [ ] **Time/cash** — Can the founder sustain the runway to reach break-even or first milestone?
- [ ] **Risks** — Top 2–3 things that could kill the business or make it not worth it.
- [ ] **Honest take** — One sentence: "This plan is realistic if …" or "This is risky because …"

If the idea has **significant headwinds** (e.g. saturated market, no validation, capital-intensive, regulatory risk), say so clearly and suggest what would need to change or what to validate before going all-in.
