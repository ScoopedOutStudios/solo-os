# PR-FAQ Reference: Watch-Outs and Process

Use this when you need deeper context on the Working Backwards PR-FAQ process or when refining a draft.

## Common Mistakes

**Selling vs. truth-seeking** — The goal is to evaluate the idea, not only to get approval. In the Internal FAQ, explicitly ask: "What needs to be true for this to succeed?" and "What are the top reasons this could fail?"

**Ignoring competition** — If the problem is real, customers already have a solution (even if it's "do nothing"). State what they use today and why they would switch to this product.

**Customer = "everyone"** — Strong PR-FAQs target a specific segment (e.g. "suburban families with two cars" vs. "people who drive"). Narrow the subheading and problem to that segment.

**Skills-forward** — Starting from "what we can build" leads to me-too products. Start from customer need and work backwards to what must be built.

**Great product, wrong problem** — A clever solution to a problem customers don't care about (or won't pay for) won't build a business. Problem paragraph must describe a problem with real TAM and willingness to pay.

## Reviewer Checklist (Internal FAQ)

When reviewing a PR-FAQ, assess:

1. Is the **customer** clearly defined?
2. Is the **problem** clearly defined and meaningful (TAM, willingness to pay)?
3. Does the **solution** directly address that problem?
4. Is the product **differentiated** (better, cheaper, or faster than current alternatives)?
5. Would we expect **behavior change** (customers switching)?
6. What **constraints** (technical, legal, operational) must be solved?
7. Is **TAM and payback** large enough?

## Process Notes

- First draft can be done in a few hours; refinement and alignment take multiple reviews.
- PR-FAQ is a funnel: many ideas in, fewer approved. Use it to compare and prioritize, not to justify building everything.
- For multi-sided products (e.g. marketplace), consider separate PR-FAQs per customer type (e.g. buyer vs. seller).
- Once the PR-FAQ is approved, use normal product/engineering process (e.g. Agile) to build; the doc is the north star, not the sprint backlog.

Template and principles are based on the Working Backwards PR/FAQ process (Amazon; see workingbackwards.com). Adopted by many companies (e.g. Stripe, Notion, Shopify) for product discovery and alignment.
